Research Vision

The question is not whether string theory contains gravity. It does. The question is how string theory organizes gravity. General Relativity successfully geometrizes the metric. The central question is therefore: What geometry describes the complete gravitational sector of closed strings?

Working Hypothesis Double Field Theory takes the generalized metric \(\mathcal H_{AB}\) and the DFT dilaton \(d\) as its fundamental geometric fields. Whenever the generalized DFT geometry admits a Riemannian parametrization, it is described by the familiar closed-string fields \(\{g_{\mu\nu},B_{\mu\nu},\phi\}\). More generally, the same DFT geometry also admits genuinely non-Riemannian realizations. Our current working hypothesis is that Double Field Theory provides the gravitational framework naturally implied by closed string theory.

Einstein Double Field Equation\(G_{AB}=T_{AB}\)An \(\mathrm{O}(D,D)\)-covariant field equation for the generalized metric and the DFT dilaton, encompassing both Riemannian and non-Riemannian geometries.

The Einstein Double Field Equation

\[ G_{AB}=T_{AB}\,, \]

is formulated directly for the generalized metric \(\mathcal H_{AB}\) and the DFT dilaton \(d\), without assuming a Riemannian parametrization.

Whenever the generalized metric admits a Riemannian parametrization, the single \(\mathrm{O}(D,D)\)-covariant equation reproduces the following coupled equations for \(g_{\mu\nu}\), \(B_{\mu\nu}\), and \(\phi\):

\[ \begin{aligned} R_{\mu\nu}+2\trd_{\mu}(\partial_{\nu}\phi)-\quarter H_{\mu\rho\sigma}H_{\nu}{}^{\rho\sigma} &\;=\; K_{(\mu\nu)}\,,\\[0.7em] \half e^{2\phi}\trd^{\rho}\!\left(e^{-2\phi}H_{\rho\mu\nu}\right)&\;=\; K_{[\mu\nu]}\,,\\[0.7em] R+4\Box\phi-4\partial_{\mu}\phi\partial^{\mu}\phi-\textstyle{\frac{1}{12}}H_{\lambda\mu\nu}H^{\lambda\mu\nu}&\;=\; \To\,. \end{aligned} \]

For genuinely non-Riemannian generalized metrics, the same Einstein Double Field Equation remains well defined even though no ordinary invertible spacetime metric exists.
