Research Vision

The question is not whether string theory contains gravity. It does. The question is how string theory organizes gravity. Closed strings contain the universal massless sector \(\{g,\,B,\,\phi\}\). General Relativity successfully geometrizes the metric. The central question is therefore: What geometry describes the full closed-string gravitational multiplet?

Working Hypothesis The current working hypothesis is that Double Field Theory provides the natural geometric framework for the universal closed-string massless sector.

The Einstein Double Field Equation

\[ G_{AB}=T_{AB}\,, \]

unifies the field equations of the complete closed-string massless sector.

Whenever the generalized metric admits a Riemannian parametrization, it reduces to the following coupled equations:

\[ \begin{aligned} R_{\mu\nu}+2\trd_{\mu}(\partial_{\nu}\phi)-\quarter H_{\mu\rho\sigma}H_{\nu}{}^{\rho\sigma} &\;=\; K_{(\mu\nu)}\,,\\[0.7em] \half e^{2\phi}\trd^{\rho}\!\left(e^{-2\phi}H_{\rho\mu\nu}\right)&\;=\; K_{[\mu\nu]}\,,\\[0.7em] R+4\Box\phi-4\partial_{\mu}\phi\partial^{\mu}\phi-\textstyle{\frac{1}{12}}H_{\lambda\mu\nu}H^{\lambda\mu\nu}&\;=\; \To\,. \end{aligned} \]
