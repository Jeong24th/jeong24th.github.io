Jeong-Hyuck Park Research Program

Gravity of Closed Strings

A Research Program

What is the gravitational theory naturally implied by closed string theory?

Research is driven by questions, not by calculations.

This document formulates a problem before naming a theory. It is written for researchers and students who wish to think carefully about the gravitational content of the universal massless sector of closed string theory.

Program Order

The purpose of the Research Program is to make the order explicit: observation, question, working hypothesis, research program, tests, open problems, and invitation.

The Question

The central question and the universal closed-string massless sector \( \{g_{\mu\nu}, B_{\mu\nu}, \phi\} \).

Research Vision

Hypothesis A, Hypothesis B, and the logical gap between General Relativity and closed-string gravity.

Geometry

Doubled-yet-gauged spacetime, connection, curvature, covariant derivatives, and non-Riemannian structures.

Field Equation

The Einstein Double Field Equation \(G_{AB}=T_{AB}\) as a proposed field equation of closed-string gravity.

Propagation

Universal \(O(D,D)\)-covariant propagation, the box operator, and gravitational waves.

Phenomenology

Post-Newtonian gravity, cosmology possibly without a dark sector, black holes, wormholes, and tests.

Open Problems

Mathematical and physical tests, missing ingredients, and falsifiable directions.

Outlook

The program remains intentionally unfinished. Its future depends on the people who choose to work on it.

Scope

This research program concerns the gravitational theory implied by the universal massless sector of perturbative closed string theory. It does not attempt to formulate the geometry of M-theory or of exceptional extensions. Nor does it assume that a Riemannian formulation is the most general description of the closed-string gravitational sector.

Within this scope, Double Field Theory is treated as the working geometric framework to be tested.
